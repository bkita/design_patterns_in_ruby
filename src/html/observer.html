Observer pattern
The Observer pattern allows you to build components that know about the activities of other components without having to tightly couple everything together in an unmanageable mess of code-flavored spaghetti. By creating a clean interface between the source of the news (the observable object) and the consumer of that news (the observers), the Observer pattern moves the news without tangling things up. Most of the work in implementing the Observer pattern occurs in the subject or observable class. In Ruby, we can factor that mechanism out into either a superclass or (more likely) a module. The interface between observer and observable can be a complex as you like, but if you are building a simple observer, code blocks work well.
